# Study Hub – Quick Start Guide

A simple guide to finding your way around the MANCOSA Study Hub. No tech skills needed.

---

## What is this?
An online study portal for four subjects: **OB, IKM, OM and MM.**
For each subject you get teaching **notes for every unit**, theory cards, worked
past-paper answers, cases and exam tools — all in one place.

## Getting around (the menu on the left)
Everything starts from the **menu on the left of the screen.**

- At the top are general links (like **Home**).
- Below that, under **Subjects**, you see the four subjects. **Click a subject** to open it.
- Each subject opens a set of tools (Units, Theories, Cases, Past Papers and more).

> On a phone: tap the **menu icon (☰)** in the top corner to open this menu.

If you ever feel lost, click **Home** (top of the menu) to start again.

## The 5 things you'll use most

1. **Units – start here for any topic.**
   Click a subject → **Units** → pick a unit. **Every unit in all four subjects** now
   opens with a plain-language **"Unit notes (start here)"** box that:
   - introduces the unit and why it matters,
   - explains each key idea simply, with an everyday example,
   - shows **how it links to the theories**, and
   - gives a table mapping each **learning outcome → module content → theory** to use.
   You can understand the whole unit from this box before opening anything else.

2. **Theories – the frameworks and models.**
   Click a subject → **Theories** → click any card. Each card explains the theory
   simply, with a real example, a South African example, and the mistakes to avoid.

3. **Past Papers – real exam questions with model answers.**
   Click a subject → **Past Papers** → pick a question → read the **Worked answer**
   to see a full, structured answer you can learn from.

4. **Cases – practise applying what you've learned** to a scenario, step by step.

5. **Exam tools** (mainly OM) – Formula cards, calculators and an answer builder
   to help you write proper exam answers.

## How to actually study with it (simple routine)
1. Pick your subject from the left menu.
2. Open **Units** and read the **Unit notes (start here)** box for your topic.
3. Click into the **Theories** linked in the unit to go deeper.
4. Go to **Past Papers**, try a question yourself first, then check the **Worked answer.**
5. Compare your answer to the model: structure, examples and references.

## Quick tips
- **Coloured/underlined text is clickable** — it takes you somewhere useful.
- Use the **"← Back"** links or **Home** if you get lost.
- Start with **Unit notes** when a topic is new; use **Past Papers** when revising.
- You don't have to read everything — jump straight to the unit or question you need.

That's it. Open a subject, read a unit's notes, try a past paper. You'll get the hang of it quickly.
